<p><p>The car engine camshaft market is primarily divided into two types: flat-tappet cams and roller cams. Flat-tappet cams feature a simple design with a flat profile, commonly used in older engine models due to their cost-effectiveness. In contrast, roller cams utilize small rollers that reduce friction, enabling more precise valve timing and improved performance, making them popular in high-performance and modern engines. The choice between these cam types significantly impacts engine efficiency, longevity, and overall performance.</p></p>
