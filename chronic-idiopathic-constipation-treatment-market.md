<p><p>Chronic idiopathic constipation treatment encompasses various diagnostic tests aiding in identifying underlying causes. Blood tests assess metabolic or hormonal imbalances. Sigmoidoscopy and colonoscopy provide visual insights into the colon's condition. The balloon expulsion test evaluates rectal function, while anorectal manometry measures anal sphincter pressure and rectal sensation. Colonic transit studies gauge bowel motility, and defecography assesses how well the body expels stool. Collectively, these tests help tailor treatment strategies for effective management of the condition.</p></p>
