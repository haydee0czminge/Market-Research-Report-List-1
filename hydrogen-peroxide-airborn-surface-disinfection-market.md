<p><p>The hydrogen peroxide airborne surface disinfection market is primarily segmented into vaporized and fogging types. Vaporized disinfection involves generating aerosolized hydrogen peroxide, allowing it to penetrate surfaces and neutralize pathogens effectively. This method is suitable for large areas and critical environments, providing uniform distribution. In contrast, fogging disinfection utilizes a fogging machine to create fine droplets of hydrogen peroxide, ensuring extensive coverage of surfaces and air. Both types are increasingly adopted in healthcare, food processing, and other sanitation-sensitive sectors for their efficacy.</p></p>
